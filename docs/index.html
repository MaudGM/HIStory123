About the HIStory project

About the HIStory project The HIStory digitizing project aims to digitize and present the rescued personal archive of a French priest who received his religious education during the 20s and 30s, was made prisoner of war during World War Two (WWII) and who lived the rest of his life in Brittany, France. HIStory argues for the importance of personal archives as an excellent tool to shed new light and perspectives on history and other transdisciplinary academic subjects. For the current project, a very limited selection of items has been made available online. It is understood that the potential meanings at stake rely as much in the association of archival items than in a single item and its content. Indeed, the association of a kept newspaper and a letter may bring much more meaning than those two same items considered separately. The ambition would be to digitize and make available related personal archives to constitute a pool and tool designed to facilitate further interpolated research. While far from being a digital scholarly edition (Sahle, 2014, 2016), it could potentially become so. In its modest manifestation, the project ambition intersects with the ÉGO (Centre de Recherche d'Histoire Quantitative) project that digitizes individual stories from World War Two in France. Digitization is not only the alternative to analogue. Indeed, the use of, for instance, Text Encoding Initiative allows for different ways to view, comment upon, and analyse the same source. While acknowledging the source in multiple ways, the digitization process may create a new and somewhat different object (See Björk, 2015). For this project the document was considered as both object and content carrier and the aim was to offer a diplomatic and a reading view of each item.
